Consortium MEL Systems

Multi-partner programs have unique MEL challenges. I fix them.

When multiple agencies share one MEL framework or set of indicators, the failure modes are predictable: indicator definitions drift across partners, data collection methods vary and results can't be meaningfully compared, no one owns quality. I specialize in building the governance structures that prevent this.

  • Indicator governance — definitions, ownership, change control, escalation paths
  • Methodology harmonization across partners with different systems and capacity levels
  • Survey question review and harmonization — aligning instruments so data is actually comparable across partners
  • Aggregation logic and data quality rules that actually hold
  • Decision-facing reporting: steering packs, summary logic, narrative that forces interpretation
  • Learning integration — AARs and learning questions mapped back to indicators

Recent examples: $100M Ukraine Cash Consortium (Mercy Corps), €19M Yemen durable solutions program (6-agency, ACTED/EU).
